Our Game

Its Story

Welcome to "Moonlight Ira", a sandbox game in which you play as an astronaut lost on an unknown and hostile planet. You'll discover a futuristic city at its center, where you'll have to survive. The environment is dangerous and challenging, so be careful!

While trying to survive, you can embark on exciting quests to help the inhabitants of this strange planet.By completing them, you'll receive "Moonies" that you can exchange for new weapons or oxygen, which represents your health bar.

Are you ready to rise to the challenge in this strange city ?

Download the game now and find out if you have what it takes to survive on a hostile planet filled with intelligent inhabitants !

Gameplay

Moonlight Ira is an action-adventure video game played in 3/4 view in an HD-2D style. In this sandbox, players are free to explore the map as they please, completing missions in any order they wish.

In this futuristic city, some characters have a strange bubble above their head. Talk to them, and they will give you a mission to complete ! By bringing them the item they wanted, you will get "Moonies", which can be exchanged for better weapons. At the beginning of your journey, you start with your bare hands, but who knows, you may end up with special weapons.

Moonlight Ira is also a cooperative experience, in which a player can invite 3 of his friends to do as they please in this space city. Filled with intelligent inhabitants capable of interacting with the player(s), you will never get bored !

Ressources

Godot Engine

We decided to use the Godot Engine, rather than Unity, for many reasons :

The first is its lightness. As a game engine, both for the designer and the player, Godot is much lighter than Unity. The result is lighter and, above all, more powerful games. Where a Unity installation takes up several gigabytes of space, a Godot installation takes up only a few hundred megabytes.

The second is its design. Although both engines share C# support, 2D and 3D capabilities, and strong community support, their paradigms differ. Whereas Unity's design encourages the creation of "all-powerful", less optimized objects, Godot encourages interaction between objects to avoid redundancy.

Last but not least, Godot boasts powerful built-in tools (notably for object animation) that will greatly benefit our project, and which are unrivalled by anything offered by Unity.

Rider

Rider is a code editor that runs on Windows, macOS and Linux. It lets you edit, generate, run and debug Unity and .NET Core applications.

As we're used to using it at school, it was an obvious choice for our project.

GitHub

GitHub is an open-source version management program. A set of files can be stored there while keeping track of the chronology of modifications made to them. Free accounts are available for open-source projects.

To organize our group work, we decided to use GitHub. As we were already used to using Git at school, it didn't take us long to get the hang of it. Above all, we had to understand how branches work. These allow you to isolate your work from other branches while you implement a new feature or simply fix a bug.

Aseprite

Aseprite is a charged image editor designed for drawing and animating pixel art. It offers a large range of tools, such as layers, frames and tilemaps, to simplify its use.

Aseprite helped us create assets and animations, like logos (for our team and Moonlight Ira), characters, various floors, weapons, dialogue bubbles, etc.

Blender

Blender is free software for 3D modeling, animation and rendering. It offers various advanced features such as 3D sculpting, texturing and more.

For our project, Blender enabled us to model buildings for the futuristic city in which the character moves, as well as trees, billboards, street lamps, etc. All these elements add depth to our game.

Ableton Live 10 Suite

Ableton Live is a professional software music sequencer for macOS and Windows. Live is a tool for composing and arranging music, but its design and ergonomics are primarily intended for live performance.

Here, we've used it to create various music tracks for our project.

Audacity

Audacity is a free and open-source digital audio editor and recording application software. It is available for Windows, macOS and Linux.

We used this application to create the sound effects in our game : the walking sound, all the sounds when using the weapons, etc.
